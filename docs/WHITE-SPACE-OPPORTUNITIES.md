# White-Space AI-Native Vertical SaaS Opportunities

**Three-agent research synthesis · June 2026**

Three independent agents (compliance-heavy field services / document-heavy professional services / long-tail SMB niches) scanned 50+ candidate verticals for the pattern that worked in fire protection: expert judgment + regulated output document + aging workforce + existing software = generic CRUD.

---

## The Winning Pattern

Wins concentrate in workflows that share **five traits**:

1. **Field or site visit** feeds the work
2. **Structured deliverable** (a report, certification, brief, memo, packet)
3. **Citable regulatory framework** (NFPA, AWWA, NPMA-33, USCIS, IRC, ASME, etc.)
4. **Aging domain experts** — replacement labor isn't catching up
5. **Existing software is CRUD or Excel** — no AI-native incumbent

Where any of those five is missing, generic field-service software wins (Jobber, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro for trades; Clio, MyCase for pro services).

# The Top 10 White-Space Opportunities

Ranked across all three research streams by **(market size × AI-moat opportunity × ease of cold-pilot)**.

## 1. Backflow preventer testing (cross-connection control)

**The cleanest transfer of your existing fire-protection playbook.**

- **Market**: 40,000-60,000 certified testers in the US. Test fees $89-$287 commercial. Mostly 1-5 person shops doing 500-3,000 tests/year. ANSI-accredited tester cert (BPAT) required in most jurisdictions post-2025.
- **Regulatory anchor**: AWWA M14, EPA SDWA, USC (Foundation for Cross-Connection Control), local water purveyor ordinances
- **Incumbents**: Tokay (Aquatic Informatics), Syncta, BSI Online, BRYCER, iWorQ — **all utility-side compliance portals**. Tester-side experience is still gauge readings on paper, then re-keyed at night.
- **AI penetration**: Low. Form-digitization at best.
- **The 10x**: Photo-of-gauge OCR → AWWA test report → auto-routed to the right utility's required form template (every utility wants its own variant) → submitted to right portal (BSI vs Tokay vs TCE vs utility email)
- **Why it's the best transfer**: Same exact AHJ-fragmentation moat as fire. Annual recurring test. Customer pain: "endless paperwork" verbatim in reviews. Owner-operator buyer profile matches.

## 2. Welding inspection daily reports (CWI / AWS)

**Zero AI competitors. Highest per-firm pain.**

- **Market**: ~22,000 active AWS Certified Welding Inspectors, ~3,000-4,000 independent CWI firms. $250K-$5M revenue each.
- **Regulatory anchor**: AWS D1.1, ASME Section IX, API 1104
- **Current software**: **There's literally a paperback "Welding Inspector Daily Report Logbook" sold on Amazon.** Pen and paper for the deliverable. ProjectVu/WeldEye exist for big EPC owners, not independents.
- **AI penetration**: **Zero for the human CWI's deliverable.** All welding AI is computer vision for factory robotic-weld defect detection — doesn't serve the inspector writing a DIR.
- **The 10x**: Voice DIR on-site → code-cited (D1.1 §6.x) report with photos, weld map, NCR flagging, signature block → auto-sent to GC's document control. **4 hours of typing per day → 20 minutes.**
- **GTM**: AWS Show, CWI online forums. The buyer is an aging former welder who already knows ChatGPT can't read AWS D1.1.
- **TAM math**: 3,500 firms × $500/mo × 50% penetration = $10M+ ARR realistic ceiling.

## 3. Probate & estate administration

**Largest demographic wave of any niche on this list.**

- **Market**: 70M boomers, $78T wealth, projected **3.6M annual deaths by 2037**. 40% of law firm partners expect to retire this decade.
- **Regulatory anchor**: 50 state probate codes + Uniform Probate Code + IRC §6166/§2032A + state-specific inventory and accounting forms
- **Incumbents**: EstateExec, Estateably, WealthCounsel, Smokeball, LEAP. The probate-software trade press literally admits these are still **"digital filing cabinets."**
- **AI penetration**: Low. Drafting layer is generic templates.
- **The 10x**: State-specific automated petition + accounting generation. Ingests bank statements, brokerage 1099s, appraisals → court-ready inventory, accounting, final-report PDFs with statutory citations.
- **Moat**: 50 state procedural-rule variations + per-county evidentiary preferences = generalist AI fails. The corpus of approved petitions per county is non-public and learnable per-jurisdiction.
- **Buyer**: solo and small-firm estate attorneys ($2K-$10K per estate). Trust departments at community banks also pay.

## 4. Commercial kitchen exhaust cleaning (NFPA 96)

**Direct adjacency to your existing fire work — same fire marshals.**

- **Market**: 5,000-8,000 hood cleaning contractors. 750k+ commercial kitchens need quarterly-to-annual service. $300-$1,500 average ticket.
- **Regulatory anchor**: **NFPA 96** (literally another NFPA standard), IKECA C10, local fire marshal AHJs, insurance carrier rules (FM Global stricter than code)
- **Incumbents**: HoodCleaningSoftware, Joyfill (generic form). Mostly **a paper sticker on the hood + a carbon-copy certificate.**
- **AI penetration**: Low. NFPA 96 explicitly still allows paper records.
- **The 10x**: Photo-in, certificate-out. Tech photographs hood/duct/fan before & after; AI generates NFPA 96 certificate including grease depth OCR'd from depth gauge, flags inaccessible areas, pushes copies to fire marshal + insurance carrier + restaurant.
- **Bonus moat**: Insurance carrier (FM Global, Travelers) integrations — if you become the source of truth for "is this kitchen compliant," carriers will route work to you.

## 5. Chimney sweep Level 2 inspection reports (NFPA 211)

**Zero AI competitors. Mandatory at every real estate transfer.**

- **Market**: ~5,000-7,000 US firms, ~$1.4B revenue, no firm >5% share. Classic owner-operator: 1-5 trucks.
- **Regulatory anchor**: NFPA 211 (Level 1 visual, **Level 2 video/camera scope mandatory at property sale**, Level 3 destructive)
- **Incumbents**: QuickBooks + Jobber/Housecall Pro. Reports typed in Word by owner's spouse at 9 PM.
- **AI penetration**: **Essentially zero. Not a single AI-native chimney inspection tool found.**
- **The 10x**: Voice + scope-camera video → NFPA 211-compliant Level 2 report with annotated stills, citations to code sections, repair quote attached. **Owner narrates while pulling the camera out.**
- **GTM**: NCSG (National Chimney Sweep Guild) conference, CSIA cert holders, real estate agent referrals. **Buyers searchable on Google Maps; you can call 50 in a day.**

## 6. Immigration law — RFE / EB-1A / NIW brief writing

**Form-fill is done. The brief layer is wide open.**

- **Market**: 18,417 immigration law businesses in the US (IBISWorld 2026), $9.9B market, 4.6% YoY growth. Overwhelmingly solo and 2-10 attorney firms.
- **Regulatory anchor**: USCIS Policy Manual, INA, 8 CFR, AAO precedent decisions
- **Incumbents**: Docketwise, Filevine ImmigrationAI, INSZoom, LollyLaw, Imagility, LegistAI. **They handle form-fill and OCR (passport, I-94) — they don't write the brief that actually wins the case.**
- **AI penetration**: Medium-high for forms; **wide open for persuasive briefs** (EB-1A "extraordinary ability," NIW "national interest," asylum declarations, RFE responses).
- **The 10x**: AAO-grounded brief writer. Kazarian / Dhanasar framework citations. Trained on tens of thousands of approved petitions.
- **Moat**: AAO decisions are public but unindexed. The training set is the moat.
- **Buyer**: solos and 2-10 attorney shops doing $3K-$15K flat-fee work. They'll switch for one good RFE-response feature.

## 7. Property tax appeals (commercial)

**Contingency economics. Per-county procedural moat.**

- **Market**: Highly fragmented. Ryan LLC dominates enterprise; thousands of regional firms (Texas Protax, Marvin F. Poer, Popp Hutcheson, hundreds smaller). Fees 30-50% of first-year tax savings.
- **Regulatory anchor**: State assessment statutes, county ARB rules, mass-appraisal methodology (cost/income/sales comparison), USPAP
- **Incumbents**: CSC AppealTrack, TaxNetUSA QuickAppeal, AppealPal, Avalara Property Tax. **Compliance calendars and comp-pulling — not intelligence layers.**
- **AI penetration**: Low. Comp-pulling is being automated; the persuasive valuation argument is not.
- **The 10x**: Auto-generated income-approach valuation memo from rent rolls + operating statements + market comps + assessor's own model. Cited comparable selections + statutory rebuttal language tuned to each county's ARB.
- **Moat**: County-level ARB procedural and evidentiary preferences. Historical settlement data from prior appeals.

## 8. Sign installation + municipal permit research

**Permit data is the moat.**

- **Market**: ~5,000-8,000 specialty sign install/fab firms. NY signage alone is $2.5B. Each US city has distinct sign codes.
- **Pain**: Permitting eats **30-40% of admin time per job** (sign + electrical + structural + wind-load permits each).
- **Incumbents**: shopVOX, Cyrious, Printavo — none handle permitting research. PermitFlow exists but is GC-focused, not sign-specific.
- **AI penetration**: Very low.
- **The 10x**: Address + sign spec → instant municipal code lookup (height, area, illumination, zoning overlay) → pre-filled permit packets → structural calcs flagged for engineer review.
- **Moat**: Per-AHJ sign-code index. **Start in TX or FL, top 50 cities, sell at $300/mo.**

## 9. FDA 510(k) submissions for class II devices

**High dollar per document. Aging consultant bench.**

- **Market**: 4,049 510(k) submissions in FY2025, +12.8% YoY. Solo consultants $15-40K per submission, firms $30-80K, law firms $50-150K.
- **Regulatory anchor**: 21 CFR 807, FDA guidance, eSTAR template, recognized consensus standards, predicate device matching
- **Incumbents**: Greenlight Guru (QMS), Cruxi (newer AI-first), Complizen. Big medtech experimenting with private LLMs. **The long tail of class II startups has no platform.**
- **AI penetration**: Low-medium and accelerating. Predicate search and standards citation are being automated; **substantial-equivalence narrative is still hand-written.**
- **The 10x**: Predicate-matching + substantial-equivalence narrative generator. Device description + bench data → eSTAR-ready submission with statute + guidance citations.
- **Moat**: FDA's cleared-510(k) corpus is public-but-unstructured. Clean training set + Q-sub history.

## 10. TAB (Test, Adjust, Balance) HVAC reports

**Smaller market, premium pricing, no incumbent.**

- **Market**: ~1,500 certified TAB firms in US. Premium-priced reports **$5k-$50k per project.**
- **Regulatory anchor**: NEBB and AABC certification standards, rigid form templates
- **Incumbents**: Bluerithm (commissioning-focused, not TAB-native), Excel templates from NEBB/AABC. **No category leader.**
- **AI penetration**: Very low.
- **The 10x**: Voice/photo capture of manometer readings at each diffuser → auto-populated AABC/NEBB report with deltas vs design CFM + auto-generated narrative explaining adjustments.
- **Caveat**: Smaller TAM (~$2-5M ARR ceiling) — better as a high-margin focused business than venture-scale.

## The Three Strategic Categories

The top 10 cluster into three distinct plays:

### Category A — Adjacent transfer (lowest risk, fastest demo)

You're already most of the way there. Same regulatory shape, same buyer profile, same AHJ moat.

- **Backflow preventer testing** — closest match
- **Commercial kitchen exhaust cleaning** — same fire marshal AHJs
- **TAB HVAC reports** — same shop-style buyer

If you want **the fastest possible second product**, backflow is the answer. Same playbook. Same code. Different namespace.

### Category B — Pure white space (highest moat, longest pilot)

No AI-native competitor exists. Zero. You define the category.

- **Welding inspection (CWI)** — best of these
- **Chimney sweep Level 2** — fastest to validate
- **Sign installation + permits** — requires permit data work
- **Powder coating quoting** — small TAM, niche pain

If you want **a defensible second business that doesn't compete with fire**, welding inspection is the cleanest shot.

### Category C — High-value professional services (highest ACV, hardest GTM)

Bigger dollar deals but harder sales motion. Lawyers and consultants don't switch software easily.

- **Probate & estate administration** — best demographic wave
- **Immigration brief writing** — best wedge against incumbents
- **Property tax appeals** — best contingency economics
- **FDA 510(k)** — highest per-document value

If you want **a venture-scale shot with a real demographic tailwind**, probate is the answer.

## What I'd Actually Do

The honest read from three independent agents:

1. **If you want to maximize your fire-protection work's reusability**: build **backflow** as product #2. Same code 80% reusable. Same buyer 80% similar. Same TCE/AHJ moat. 6-month timeline.

2. **If you want a defensible second business that doesn't conflict with fire**: build **welding inspection daily reports**. Zero AI competitors, ~3,500 firms with $4-hour-per-day report pain, owner-operator buyer who'll respond to a phone call. 9-month timeline.

3. **If you want a venture-scale moonshot**: build **probate**. Largest demographic wave of any niche on this list. 50-state regulatory variation is moat-grade. The aging-out problem is so severe it's existential to the industry. 18-month timeline.

The pattern across all three: **build for an aging owner who can't find junior help, ground the AI in jurisdiction-specific regulation, and own one piece of the regulatory submission pipeline.** That's the AI-native vertical-SaaS thesis in one sentence.

## Honest Caveats

- **All three agents flagged "AI is moving fast."** EvenUp's $1B PI chronology valuation is the cautionary tale: as soon as a category becomes obvious, it gets eaten in 18 months. The 10 verticals above will all have AI-native competitors by 2028. The window is now.
- **"Number of firms" is not "addressable market."** A 3,500-firm vertical with $500/mo ACV is a $20M ARR ceiling — real but not venture-scale. The bigger plays (probate, immigration, fire, backflow) are >$100M ceilings.
- **Cold outreach matters.** The cleanest test: pick a vertical, pull 50 owner emails from Google Maps + LinkedIn, send a 100-word "I built this thing, can I show it to you?" email. Response rate >5% = real demand. <2% = wrong fit.
- **Generalist AI is closing in on some of these.** Property tax appeals, immigration form-fill, R&D credits — all increasingly tractable for GPT-4-class models directly. The defensible plays are where jurisdiction-specific data and procedural rules dominate.
